eBoto MMCM is a blockchain-based voting application and system designed to ensure secure, transparent, and tamper-proof SSG elections within Mapúa Malayan Colleges Mindanao.
By integrating blockchain technology, eBoto MMCM promotes trust and integrity in the electoral process, empowering students to vote with confidence anytime, anywhere within the campus.
NIC001, the team of developers behind eBoto MMCM, are former Grade 12 STEM students of Mapúa Malayan Colleges Mindanao, consisting of: (1) Mark Jian P. Benaning, (2) Jade B. Dela Cruz, (3) Joakim Josef G. Famador, (4) Erick John T. Fronteras, (5) James A. Gigantana, (6) Myle Genesis L. Medenilla, (7) Aiden Kurt Monarca, (8) Zimri B. Quillano. They created the system to address common issues in traditional school elections, such as lack of transparency, manual errors, and limited accessibility. Inspired by the need for a more secure and trustworthy voting process, they turned to blockchain technology to ensure that every vote is recorded accurately and cannot be altered. By building eBoto MMCM, the team aims to promote student engagement, uphold election integrity, and modernize the way Mapúa Malayan Colleges Mindanao conducts its elections.
